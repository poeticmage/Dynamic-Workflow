## 3. Conduct Technical Interview

**Technical Interview - Senior Backend Software Engineer**
**Candidate:** Alice Smith
**Instructions to Interviewer:** Aim for a conversational yet structured approach. Allow elaboration. Dig deeper. Note problem-solving, technical depth, communication, leadership potential.

**I. Welcome & Introduction (5 minutes)**
1.  "Hi Alice, thanks for joining us today. My name is [Your Name] and I'm a [Your Role] here. We'll spend the next [60-75] minutes discussing your experience and diving into some technical topics relevant to the Senior Backend Software Engineer role. Please feel free to ask questions at any point. To start, could you give us a brief overview of your career path and what you're looking for in your next role?"

**II. Core Backend Development & Language Proficiency (20 minutes)**
1.  **Python & RESTful APIs:** Discuss a complex RESTful API designed with Python. Key design considerations for scalability, security, maintainability, API versioning. Follow-up on 30% throughput improvement: specific architectural patterns/optimizations in Python/Flask.
2.  **Go Language:** Describe experience with Go, projects (personal or professional). Strengths/weaknesses compared to Python for backend services. Follow-up (if limited Go experience): Approach to learning and contributing effectively if a new project required Go.

**III. Database Expertise (10 minutes)**
1.  **Relational & NoSQL:** Discuss a scenario for choosing between relational (PostgreSQL) and NoSQL (MongoDB) databases. Factors, trade-offs.
2.  **Database Optimization:** Describe diagnosing/optimizing a slow query or bottleneck in PostgreSQL/MongoDB. Tools, techniques, outcome.

**IV. Cloud, Containerization & CI/CD (15 minutes)**
1.  **AWS Services:** Describe a project leveraging multiple AWS services (EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda). Benefits and challenges of that architecture.
2.  **Docker & Kubernetes:** Elaborate on practical Docker experience (containerizing, Dockerfiles). Exposure to Kubernetes (deploying/managing in supporting role). If not, how to gain proficiency. Follow-up on 50% deployment time reduction with CI/CD, detailing the Jenkins pipeline and Docker's role.
3.  **CI/CD Practices:** Thoughts on best practices for CI/CD in microservices. Ensuring quick, reliable deployments, code quality, minimizing risks.

**V. System Design & Architecture (15 minutes)**
1.  **Microservices Design:** Design a new notification service (email, SMS, push) integrating external providers from a microservices perspective. Components, communication.
    *   *Follow-up:* Ensuring reliability, fault tolerance, idempotency in a distributed system.
    *   *Follow-up:* Handling asynchronous processing and queueing.

**VI. Behavioral & Leadership Skills (10 minutes)**
1.  **Mentorship & Collaboration:** Describe mentoring approach. Most challenging code review, navigation for positive outcome.
2.  **Problem Solving & Debugging:** Challenging production issue, diagnosis, resolution. Lessons learned.
3.  **Cross-functional Collaboration:** Approach to collaborating with product managers and other cross-functional teams for API specs and feature integration, especially with differing priorities/constraints.

**VII. Wrap-up & Candidate Questions (5 minutes)**
1.  "Do you have any questions for me about the role, our team, or the company?"
2.  "Thank you for your time, Alice. We'll be in touch soon regarding the next steps."
